Klaviyo Pricing Page Concept

4 Hour Hack Based on Founder's Talk

Andrew Bialecki, co-founder of Klaviyo came into Startup Institute for a Fireside Chat. During the Q&A session, a student asked for clarification on Klaviyo's pricing tiers. Andrew mentioned that their pricing page needed some work, so I figured this was a great opportunity to work on a problem. Andrew mentioned that he looks for 3 things in a product person:

  • 1. Synthesize Research
  • 2. Mock up a Solution
  • 3. Measure the Changes

Gathering

The work of Saas pricing in general, and pricing pages, specifically, was not in my wheelhouse. I would have to spend most of my time up front learning about pricing pages. Fortunately, Google was had plenty of info, including a lot of articles that highlighted one of Boston's most well known SaaS startups, HubSpot.

Ideate

After a crash course in learning about SaaS pricing page best practices, I had to synthesize what features would deliver the highest impact with lowest implementation costs. I grabbed a few benchmarks that could be rolled out and tested to see what would deliver the best conversion rates.

Build

Now it was time to put together a mock up. My goals were to incorporate some of the most important metrics, while ensuring the Klaviyo brand and look was kept intact and these changes could be made without development having to create new features.

Measure

While these changes might have made sense to me, they were only based on my opinion. I do not (as of yet) have access to their page analytics. Based on the changes I proposed, I offered some metrics that would help determine which proposed changes were effective.
