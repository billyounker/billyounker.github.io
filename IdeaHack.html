Boston Public Market Idea Hack

A 22-ish Hour Hackathon

Week one of Startup Institute starts off with a bang. Day 1 you're asked to share, in front of a group of strangers, what you "suck at." The week culminates with an Idea Hack. This year's partner was Boston Public Market (BPM) , an exciting new market downtown Boston that has been opened for less than a year but has been 2+ decades in the making.

Gathering

BPM invited us into their space and gave us an overview of their mission, vision, and specific constraints as a non-profit facilitating for profit businesses. They asked to help generate awareness about their brand and to attract both digital and foot traffic. BPM was happy with their own branding, but expressed the challenges of telling the BPM story aloingside the stories and brandings of the 40 unique vendors.

Our team decided to spend the first hour or so walking around the space, talking to vendors, picking up paper handouts, and asking visitors what brought them to the market.

Ideate

Our team headed back to our space at 50 Milk and began brainstorming what the biggest challenges were facing BPM. Once we had that list scoped out, we moved on to possible solutions for the problem.

Test

Part of our scope included ensuring what we recommended to BPM was implementable on their end without prohibitive costs and time. We used this parameter to immediately throw out some of the solutions we proposed. At the end of a long day, we came to the conclusion that there simply wasn't enough data to propose changes to their current website, so we instead chose to focus on something they asked for: a virtual community.

Build

Before going for home for the night, I offered to do a quick mock up. There had been lots of ideas exchanged, but pictures are worth a 1,000. Or something. I spent a few minutes early next morning putting together a mockup for the team to give feedback on.

Measure

I took measure of the groups feeling on my mockup and it wasn't high. Or long. Or warm. They each gave comments and I sketched up a new look in my notebook. Consensus was granted so I went back into high fidelity. I also took measure of the clock. With less than 4 hours to go until presentations, I suggested we put our head downs to get to work.

Refine

I summoned my past days of working on a trading floor on Wall Street and started putting together pages as quickly as possible. There wasn't enough time for feedback on designs as they went, so I took the branding and messaging feel I got from BPM and incorporated into the mockups, writing the copy as I went. All in all, not too bad for a half of days work. Thanks to the team, BPM for an awesome hackathon and the honorable mention!
