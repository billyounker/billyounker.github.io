Concept: Runkeeper Teams

Feature build based on research

A running/triathlon friend of mine mentioned I should check out Runkeeper as a possible place to work. Although I use little to no tracking data while (if) working out, I am always intrigued to learn more about intersection of data and behavior. After downloading the app I started kicking around some ideas.

Gathering

Tracking steps, running stats, etc. has become commoditized. What differentiates one app from another is often the community that engages with the platform. Runkeeper makes it relatively easy to get a feel for their users by looking at features requests and blog posts.

Ideate

After using the app, reading a whole bunch of blog posts and looking at feature requests, I wrote down 5 different ideas. Based on the existing functionality of the Runkeeper platform and looking at their business model, I came up with the idea for "Runkeeper Groups." Runkeeper's Summer 2015 rebranding was focused on including all types of runners. Based on my research, there doesn't seem to be online, coach led, support group for runners. This seems like a big area that could help reach "every" runner.

Test

Running groups are everywhere. Even Nike and Uber got together for running support at the 2016 Boston Marathon. Outside of running, group support has been validated both in research and commercially through companies like, Omada Health and (now Oprah's) Weight Watchers.

Build

I used a storyboard to help me map out what I wanted for functionality in Runkeeper teams. I started with the persona of someone who is overweight and wants to make a change, but doesn't use social media. This user needs a supportive community, but wants that community to be online, private and led by a coach.

Measure

Runkeeper is a mobile first company. Turns out running with a laptop isn't easy and the lack of GPS removes the incentive to run with a computer in the first place. I mocked up an onboarding process for mobile. I used to same style and functionality that Runkeeper currently uses for setting a plan or goal to keep things consistent. I had a handful of people run through the onboarding process to ensure they understood the purpose of this new functionality.

Refine

I felt I had enough research to support groups feature. Implementing the technology would require mostly customization of existing the platform, rather than building new functionality from scratch. The two areas that need exploring the most are: the business model and the coaching aspect. Runkeeper already offers a premium service. Based on the time I envision a coach needing to support a team, the price point would have to be higher than it currently is. Looks like these will be questions for the new team to answer!
