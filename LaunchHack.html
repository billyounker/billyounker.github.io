Wrangl - Take Charge of Your Subscriptions

4 Hour Hack with Tom Hughes

We recently had the pleasure of hosting Tom Hughes, Apple Designer, Idealab co-founder, etc. etc. etc. The session was a highlight for me, personally, and the class as a whole. It was incredible to see how much the quality of work had improved from each team in comparsion to the IdeaHack in week 1 with the Boston Public Market.

Gathering

Tom gave us 4 different areas we could work on, or, we could choose a problem of our own. After a quick discussion about market size, opportunity for innovation, and personal excitement level for each proposed problem, we discussed personal ideas. Fortunatelly, Jill shared her idea first and it resonated with us all immediately. It was a problem we all experience: how do you manage all of your monthly subscription?

Test

Once we agreed on a rough idea of what this service should include, we set aside 10 minutes for each of us in the group to sketch out visuals. From there, we went around and each shared our thought processes for look, feel navigation and basic functionality for an MVP.

Build

We had less than 4 hours to complete the Launch Hack. Since we did not have time to do formal testing, we discussed the pros and cons of each sketch to figure out what was working. We used the Jobs to Be Done Framework popularized by Intercom to help us focus in on the functionality needs of the app. We then combined the best parts of each of our sketches.

Refine

With the deadline for our presentations quickly approaching, I put my head down and went straight from sketches to high fidelity mock ups. I started with the main screen. The goal here was to give the user a snapshot of their overall monthly subscription spend. The next critical part of the app is to show a list view, with search functionality, of each subscription. This view gives the user an easy way to access and manage each subscription. With 15 - 20 minutes left, I hopped back to home screen and made a couple variations so that we could fake some animations with static visuals during the presentation.
